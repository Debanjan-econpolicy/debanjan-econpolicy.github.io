About

My work

My research sits at the intersection of public policy and development economics, with a particular focus on enterprise development and financial inclusion. I am especially interested in how targeted interventions can help micro and nano enterprises — particularly those operating in informal markets — grow, formalize, and become more resilient.

I approach these questions using rigorous field experiments and quasi-experimental designs, always with an eye toward improving both program effectiveness and the empirical methods used to evaluate them. My fieldwork spans Tamil Nadu and Gujarat, where I collaborate closely with local institutions to understand how financial inclusion and enterprise support programs can be better designed and implemented.

Beyond enterprise development, I am deeply interested in survey methodology — developing innovative interviewer training approaches that enhance data quality in large-scale household surveys. I also examine how agricultural interventions and integrated farming systems complement enterprise development initiatives, drawing on my background in agricultural extension.

Working Paper · 2024
Impact of Conditional Loan Subsidies on Small and Medium Enterprise Performance: Evidence from Tamil Nadu

Exploits quasi-experimental variation from administrative rejections and staggered rollout across 7 cohorts to identify causal effects of relaxing financial and managerial constraints on 3,000 SMEs.

This study investigates how conditional loan subsidies, paired with managerial training, affect production choices and performance of small and medium enterprises operating in credit-constrained rural markets. We exploit quasi-experimental variation generated by administrative rejections unrelated to firm fundamentals, alongside staggered rollout across seven quarterly cohorts in Tamil Nadu (2022–2024).

Using matched data for 3,000 enterprises and a staggered difference-in-differences framework, we find that alleviating constraints significantly enhances firm survival (39–40% higher), increases revenue (11.4%), and boosts profits (up to 17.9% in early cohorts). Firms shift production strategies with larger strategic investments (average investment up 57%, asset-creation investments up 90%) while maintaining cost efficiency.

Keywords: SMEs, conditional subsidies, managerial training, credit constraints, rural markets

Working Paper · 2024
Post-Pandemic Resilience and Growth: Evaluating the Impact of the Nano Enterprise Financing Fund (NEFF) on Rural Enterprises in Tamil Nadu

Analyzes enterprise-level data from 2,576 firms across 208 villages to identify how relaxing liquidity constraints reshapes the production function of informal rural nano-enterprises.

This study investigates how alleviating liquidity constraints influences the production function and performance of rural nano enterprises. Using a natural experiment with 2,576 firms across 208 villages and a difference-in-differences framework, we find improved credit access leads to significant increases in profits (0.149 SD) and revenues (0.117 SD) without proportional cost escalation.

Firms reallocate capital toward fewer, larger investments (0.298 SD) and prioritize working capital (0.156 SD) — the binding constraint on output. Substantial improvements in record-keeping (2.5 SD) suggest enhanced managerial inputs into the production process.

Keywords: Nano enterprises, liquidity constraints, financial inclusion, informal markets, rural development

Working Paper · 2024
Does Honesty Pay? Evidence from a Field Experiment on Dishonesty and Enterprise Performance in Informal Markets

Randomizes exposure to opportunities for dishonest behavior across 208 villages to causally identify how honesty affects profitability among 2,576 nano-enterprises, using an IV approach.

This study examines whether honesty influences enterprise performance in informal markets, where nano-entrepreneurs operate under weak formal enforcement and thin margins. We designed a novel field experiment involving 2,576 nano-enterprises across 208 villages in Tamil Nadu, randomizing exposure to opportunities for dishonest behavior via a mathematics task mimicking daily business calculations, and exploiting this as an instrument.

Honesty significantly improves enterprise profitability and revenue, driven by lower transaction costs and enhanced supplier and customer trust. Firms demonstrating greater honesty secured better trade terms, reduced monitoring requirements, and experienced stronger customer loyalty. Honesty acts as a price-discriminating factor in competitive informal markets.

Keywords: Honesty, informal markets, nano-enterprises, field experiments, behavioral economics

Working Paper · 2024
Data Quality and Autonomy-Supportive Agentic Feedback: Evidence from an Interviewer Training Experiment

Reports findings from an experiment in 120 Indian villages comparing three interviewer training approaches and their impact on survey data quality in large-scale household surveys.

This paper compares three approaches to survey training: agentic-feedback training (AFT), where interviewers received autonomy-supportive feedback; retesting approach (RTA); and the standard restudying approach (RSA). AFT reduced the likelihood of at least one data quality flag per survey by 36.6%, invalid missing responses by 55.3 (relative to control mean of 1,217), and valid skips by 76.25 (relative to control mean of 1,665).

Improvements were driven by reductions in interviewer effects, enhanced ownership of surveys, and better on-field problem-solving. Cost-effectiveness analysis shows AFT offered significant improvements in data quality relative to expenditure, outperforming both alternatives.

Keywords: Survey methodology, data quality, interviewer training, developing countries, field experiments
